Confere: every transaction with its document

At the end of the month, a small business has the bank statement on one side and a folder of invoices and receipts on the other. Confere, by LMsystems, crosses the two and answers the question that matters: which transaction is reconciled, which needs review, and which one is missing its document. All ready to hand to the accountant.

ReconciledMissing documentNo match

Three states run through the whole product, from the panel to the Excel report. Colour tells the owner where to look first.

The journey of a document

From a photo taken at the counter to a reconciled row on the panel, the path is always the same.

  1. Intake, whichever way it comesA photo of the receipt sent to the Telegram bot, a PDF arriving by email, or a file dropped into the watched folder. The bot confirms on the spot: "Received, queued for processing". Email is read in read-only mode; it never marks or deletes anything in the mailbox.
  2. ClassificationEach file is identified as a bank statement or a fiscal document and goes to the right queue. The watched folder waits for the file to finish copying before touching it.
  3. Laddered extractionFirst the tax authority QR code, mandatory on Portuguese invoices. Failing that, the PDF text; then OCR; and only as a last resort AI vision, running locally, with a second model reviewing doubtful cases and a hard cap on calls. Cheap to expensive: only the necessary step gets used.
  4. Exact matchingEach transaction looks for its document by amount and date, with money always in integer cents, never floating point. Above the confidence threshold it is reconciled; in doubt it goes to review. The system does not decide on its own what it is not sure about.
  5. DeliveryA local panel in the browser, a monthly Excel report, history in SQLite, and the files tidied up: processed on one side, orphans on the other.

The mechanisms underneath

  • Exact money: every amount in integer cents, end to end. The reconciliation balances to the cent or it does not balance.
  • Idempotent reconciliation: running the same month twice gives the same result. Saving by period replaces, never duplicates.
  • Extraction ladder: tax QR code, native text, OCR, AI vision. Each step is only used if the previous one fails, and vision has an A/B judge and a limited budget.
  • Tax-number check-digit validation: on the statement and on the documents, a misread tax number does not get through.
  • Per-row states: pending → reconciled / review / missing document / orphan. Documents from future months wait their turn in the queue.
  • Local-first: runs on your own machine, at 127.0.0.1. Works without internet, except to fetch email attachments. Financial data never leaves home.
  • Silent mode: a schedulable routine reconciles on its own and leaves a summary of pending items in plain language, ready to read in the morning.

Who it is for

For the business ownerTaking a photo of the receipt at the counter is enough. The rest is automatic, and closing the month stops being a lost afternoon.
For the accountantOne chore per client, multiplied by dozens. Confere delivers each month already matched: only the exceptions ask for human attention.
Several channels, one queueTelegram, email and the watched folder feed the same pipeline. However the paperwork arrives, it ends up in the same place.
Privacy by architectureStatements and invoices never go up to any cloud. The vision AI runs at home, on the same machine.
